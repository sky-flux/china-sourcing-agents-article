A-grade lithium cells (such as Samsung 18650/21700, Panasonic, Murata, or CATL prismatic) are engineered to maintain their rated capacity for 300–500 full charge cycles with less than 20% overall degradation. Conversely, B-grade cells — commonly found in budget promotional power banks — are rejected cells that failed stringent quality grading at the battery factory. While they may test at their rated capacity when brand new, they typically degrade rapidly to 60–70% capacity within 3–6 months of regular use. A corporate client who distributes 500 branded power banks as corporate gifts and receives complaints that they "stopped working" within a year will not place a reorder. Our expert sourcing service identifies and vets OEM factories that can document verifiable cell provenance and provide authentic cycle-life test data directly from the cell manufacturer.

It's crucial to understand that rated capacity is not the same as usable capacity: a 10,000 mAh portable charger equipped with B-grade cells and an inefficient DC-DC converter may deliver only 5,500 mAh to a connected smartphone (representing a poor 55% efficiency). A high-quality unit featuring A-grade cells and a well-designed, highly efficient converter delivers a robust 7,000–7,500 mAh at the output (70–75% efficiency). Always request a rigorous capacity discharge test during your sample inspection phase: fully charge the power bank, then discharge it completely through a 5V/2A dummy load and accurately measure the actual mAh delivered.

UN 38.3 certification is the globally recognized international standard for lithium battery transport — making it absolutely mandatory for international air shipment. Power banks utilized as promotional tech gifts are frequently shipped by air courier (like DHL, FedEx, or UPS) directly to corporate clients or event venues. Without proper UN 38.3 documentation, courier companies can outright reject shipments or levy substantial hazard surcharges. Our sourcing service rigorously verifies that the UN 38.3 test report explicitly covers the exact cell configuration integrated into your custom power bank — not just a generic, broadly applied cell certificate. For comprehensive quality assurance and capacity verification, our inspection service can perform advanced discharge testing on mass production samples to confidently confirm A-grade cell performance.
