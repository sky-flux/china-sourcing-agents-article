A smart downlight is a recessed ceiling fixture that combines a directional LED module with wireless control, typically over Tuya Wi-Fi, Zigbee 3.0, or BLE mesh. RGBCW versions add a tunable-white core plus an RGB ring for accent lighting, all in a compact housing that fits standard Ø75mm, Ø90mm, or Ø150mm ceiling cut-outs. Because downlights are installed in large quantities per project — often hundreds or thousands of units — even a small per-unit failure rate produces significant warranty exposure, making driver and wireless-module consistency critical.

Smart downlights are the highest-volume fixture in a Gulf villa or apartment fit-out, and the single most common Saudi rejection point is efficiency: SASO 2902:2023 requires **≥90 lm/W**, mandatory since 2025-06-01. A common 80 lm/W RGBCW downlight that ships fine elsewhere will be rejected at SABER. This page covers what to verify on an RGBCW smart downlight sourced from China for the Saudi and GCC market.
