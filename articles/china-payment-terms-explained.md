# China Payment Terms Explained: T/T, L/C, and How Not to Get Burned

> **Read the full guide with updates**: [china-sourcing-agents.com/guides/china-payment-terms-explained](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/guides/china-payment-terms-explained)

Payment terms are where sourcing deals go wrong — not usually because of fraud, but because buyers and factories have opposite incentives and poorly structured terms leave one side holding all the risk.

---

## The core tension

Factories want money before they spend on materials and labor. Buyers want goods before they pay in full. Neither is wrong — both have legitimate business reasons. Understanding the tools gives you leverage.

---

## Telegraphic Transfer (T/T)

T/T is a direct bank wire — dominant for China sourcing because it's fast, cheap, and requires no bank infrastructure beyond a SWIFT-capable account.

### Common structures

| Structure | Buyer risk | Typical use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| 100% upfront | Very high | Sample orders only |
| 50/50 | High | Small first orders, established relationships |
| 30% deposit / 70% before shipment | High | Standard first order |
| 30% deposit / 70% against copy of B/L | Medium | **Recommended default** |

**30% deposit / 70% against copy of B/L** is the buyer-friendly variation. You pay 70% when goods are handed to the freight forwarder (evidenced by the Bill of Lading scan), not when the factory says they're ready.

### How to protect yourself on T/T

1. **Require pre-shipment inspection before releasing the balance.** Write it into your PO: "Balance T/T released upon successful pre-shipment inspection." A [third-party pre-shipment inspection](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/services/inspection) costs $200–300 and is trivial against what it protects.

2. **Use copy of B/L as the payment trigger**, not "before shipment." Goods in the carrier's hands is meaningfully different from goods the factory says are ready.

3. **Confirm bank account in writing before every wire.** Factory bank accounts change. Phone-based fraud where someone substitutes a different account number in your email chain is rare but real.

4. **Wire in USD from a major bank.** Cross-border USD wires have better dispute infrastructure than local transfers.

## Letter of Credit (L/C)

An L/C is a bank guarantee — your bank pays the factory's bank when specific documentary conditions are met. Neither you nor the factory touches the money directly.

**When L/C is appropriate:**
- Orders over $100,000 where the risk justifies the cost
- When corporate policy requires L/C above a threshold
- When the factory specifically requests it (larger factories can borrow against an L/C)

**Why most hardware startups don't use L/C:**
The cost is real: 0.5–2% of the letter value in bank fees, plus weeks of setup and significant paperwork. On a $20,000 order, that's $100–400 in fees plus friction. T/T with good inspection structure often provides comparable protection at a fraction of the cost.

**Sight L/C vs. Usance L/C:**
- *Sight L/C*: pays the factory immediately upon complying documents — what you want as a buyer
- *Usance/Term L/C*: pays 30/60/90 days after shipment — a credit facility; the factory builds the financing cost into their price

---

## Escrow (Alibaba Trade Assurance)

Trade Assurance holds your payment until delivery conditions are met, then releases to the factory. Covers refunds if goods are significantly below specification.

**Works well for:** Orders $1,000–20,000 with Alibaba-listed suppliers.

**Limitations:** Only covers purchases through Alibaba. Once you move to direct factory relationships (which you eventually should), you lose Trade Assurance. Dispute resolution takes 4–6 weeks and requires documented claims with photo evidence.

## Decision guide by order value

| Order value | Recommended terms |
|-------------|------------------|
| < $5,000 (samples) | T/T upfront. The risk is manageable on test orders. |
| $5,000–50,000 | 30% deposit / 70% against copy of B/L + pre-shipment inspection |
| $50,000–200,000 | Same + consider splitting into two shipments |
| $200,000+ | L/C or explicit escrow with documentary conditions |

---

## Currency risk

Most electronics components are quoted in USD. Lock your price in the PO: *"Price is fixed at $X USD per unit regardless of currency fluctuation."* Established factories will accept this for order windows under 90 days.
