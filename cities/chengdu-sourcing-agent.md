Engineer-led sourcing in Chengdu. Intel, Foxconn and BOE supply chain access, Chengdu–Europe rail freight advantage, gateway to Southwest China.

---

Chengdu is the capital of Sichuan province, the largest city in Southwest China by GDP, and one of the few inland Chinese cities with a genuinely diversified manufacturing base — spanning consumer electronics, automotive, industrial IoT, food processing, and a growing medical device sector. For sourcing agents, Chengdu is most relevant as the electronics hub of Western China and as the starting point for the China–Europe rail freight corridor that changes the logistics calculus for EU buyers.

The anchor tenants in Chengdu's electronics manufacturing base are household names: Intel operates a chip assembly and test facility here; Foxconn's Chengdu campus historically produced iPad assemblies and continues contract electronics manufacturing; BOE has AMOLED display panel production; Dell runs a China design and assembly center in Tianfu New Area.

These facilities are not directly accessible to independent international buyers — their capacity is committed to OEM contracts. Their value is what they created around them: a 12,000+ manufacturer ecosystem calibrated to tier-1 OEM quality expectations. Foxconn's supply chain requirements forced every nearby supplier to implement AQL inspection, FMEA documentation, and process control systems that most Pearl River Delta SME suppliers adopted only later or not at all.
