Nanning is the capital of Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region and China's designated gateway to Southeast Asia. Sitting just 160 km from the Vietnam border, it hosts the annual China-ASEAN Expo (CAEXPO) — the largest trade event bridging China and the ten ASEAN member states. Its manufacturing base is built around a growing electronics assembly cluster and China's largest sugar and agricultural processing industry. For procurement professionals managing cross-border supply chains or sourcing both industrial equipment and agricultural commodities, partnering with a Nanning sourcing agent unlocks logistics infrastructure and cost efficiencies not found in the Pearl River Delta.

Nanning's primary function in global supply chains is acting as the administrative, logistics, and customs hub for China-ASEAN cross-border trade. The China-ASEAN Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA) provides zero or near-zero tariffs on most goods traded between China and ASEAN member states. Nanning concentrates the customs infrastructure, bonded logistics zones, and freight forwarding expertise necessary for CAFTA-eligible procurement.

The annual **China-ASEAN Expo (CAEXPO)** draws over 80,000 buyers from across ASEAN and China, covering consumer electronics, agricultural products, building materials, and food processing equipment. Buyers attending the Canton Fair in Guangzhou should consider timing a Nanning factory visit around CAEXPO.
