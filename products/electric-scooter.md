OEM electric scooters from China are personal light electric vehicles built around an aluminum or steel folding frame, a brushless hub motor, a lithium-ion battery pack, and a motor controller with regenerative or electronic braking. They are ordered in 50- to 500-unit batches by shared-mobility operators, EU distributors, and commuter brands that need custom deck branding, app connectivity, and market-specific speed limiters. Because the product crosses electronics, battery logistics, and vehicle safety, the supplier base clusters around the Shenzhen and Changzhou electronics corridors. Typical lead time is 40–60 days for a 50-unit order and can stretch to 75 days if you need custom deck tooling, branded packaging, or IoT lock integration.

When partnering with an electric scooter manufacturer in China for wholesale distribution, regulatory alignment is critical. EN 17128 (Safety requirements for personal light electric vehicles — PLEVs) became a harmonised European standard in 2021 under the CE framework. It covers OEM electric scooters, electric skateboards, hoverboards, and similar custom mobility devices with a maximum speed of 25 km/h (as regulated devices in most EU member states). Key requirements for wholesale e-scooters include braking distance (maximum 7.5m from 25 km/h), stability during acceleration and braking, handlebar strength, and battery safety.

National regulations vary significantly across EU member states: in France (Arrêté du 24 octobre 2019) and Germany (eKFV), e-scooters require insurance, must use cycle paths, and are limited to 20–25 km/h. Speed-limited firmware is not the same as hardware limiting — verify the motor controller cannot be easily unlocked via app or Bluetooth. Finding a reliable e-scooter factory that meets these exact needs is paramount for importers. Our audit service verifies that the CE declaration of conformity explicitly references EN 17128 and that the speed limiting is implemented in hardware, not bypassable firmware.
