Grid operator approval is a standalone conformity procedure required, in addition to CE marking, for grid-connected electronic products in Germany. While CE marking governs free movement of goods within the EU single market, grid operator approval verifies the concrete compatibility with the German power grid — a national requirement derived from the Energy Industry Act (EnWG) and the Technical Connection Rules (TAR).

A grid operator approval is the formal confirmation by a distribution system operator (DSO, German VNB) that a product meets the technical conditions for connection to the power grid. Unlike CE marking, which rests on manufacturer self-declaration, grid operator approval requires testing by an accredited laboratory and active authorization by the DSO. Germany has around 900 distribution system operators, each with its own connection practice — though the core technical requirements are unified in the VDE-AR-N guidelines.
