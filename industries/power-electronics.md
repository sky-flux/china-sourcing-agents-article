Power electronics covers devices that convert, store, or manage electrical power — GaN chargers, power banks, battery management systems (BMS), DC-DC converters, EV charging accessories, and solar inverters — sourced from China for global markets. It is one of the most compliance-intensive categories in China sourcing: a charger pulled from an Amazon listing for a certification failure costs 5–10× more than getting compliance right upfront. Our engineering background means we can read actual test reports — not just check that a document exists. Our sourcing service covers the full process from factory shortlist to verified, audit-backed quote.

Lithium products require UN 38.3 before transport; standalone cells (PI 965) are heavily restricted on air freight, so sea freight is the practical route for volume battery shipments. Battery cell and pack production concentrates around Ningde (home to CATL) and Wenzhou, while charger and power-supply assembly clusters in the Yangtze Delta around Changzhou and Jiaxing.

**UL certification for the US market** — UL 62368-1 (covering audio/video/IT equipment including chargers) replaced UL 60950. Many factories still reference the old standard on their documentation. We verify that test reports are current and issued by NRTL-accredited labs — not just that a UL logo appears on the product.
