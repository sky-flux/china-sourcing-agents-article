# UKCA Marking Guide for Electronics: Post-Brexit Certification

> **Read the full guide**: [https://china-sourcing-agents.com/wiki/ukca-marking/](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/wiki/ukca-marking/)

Navigate UKCA marking for electronics imported from China. Learn the post-Brexit requirements, RER 2017 standards, and compliance steps for the UK market.

---

UKCA (UK Conformity Assessed) marking was introduced after the UK left the European Union single market, serving as the replacement for CE marking for products placed on the Great Britain (England, Wales, and Scotland) market. For consumer electronics importers sourcing from China — from electric kettles to TWS earphones — UKCA creates a distinct compliance obligation separate from the EU's CE.

While the technical requirements and safety standards largely mirror their EU directive counterparts, the legal framework, responsible body recognition, and market surveillance are entirely separate. Note that Northern Ireland remains under EU CE rules under the Windsor Framework.

UKCA was established under the Product Safety and Metrology etc. (Amendment etc.) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019 and subsequent instruments. The UK government's Office for Product Safety and Standards (OPSS) is the policy owner; enforcement is by Trading Standards authorities in each UK nation.
