AC-DC power modules are one of the most compliance-critical components in any electronics product. Get the wrong grade from the wrong factory, and you are looking at failed certification tests, costly re-spins, or — in the EU — customs holds. This page covers what separates competent Chinese suppliers from budget-tier gambles, and where the real compliance traps are for Western buyers.

IEC 60950-1 (the old IT equipment safety standard) was withdrawn in December 2020 for new product submissions in most markets. The replacement is IEC 62368-1, a hazard-based safety engineering (HBSE) standard that covers audio/video, IT, and communications equipment under one framework.

The practical difference for power module buyers: **a CB test report citing IEC 60950-1 is no longer valid for new CE or UL submissions**. Yet a meaningful share of Chinese power module inventory — especially from smaller Shenzhen manufacturers running legacy tooling — still carries IEC 60950-1 certificates issued before the cutoff. They are legally grandfathered for existing products in some markets but cannot be used to certify a new product for EU or US sale.
