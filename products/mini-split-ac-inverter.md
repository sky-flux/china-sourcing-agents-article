SEER2 (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio 2) replaced SEER1 as the US energy efficiency standard for residential AC equipment from January 2023. The US DOE revised the test methodology to use M1 test conditions — a higher static external pressure that more accurately reflects real ductless installation pressure drops — which results in SEER2 ratings roughly 5–7% lower than the SEER1 ratings previously assigned to the same equipment. Regional minimum requirements: SEER2 13.4 for split-system AC in the US Northern region, SEER2 15.2 in the Southern region (effective January 2023 for new manufactured equipment).

Tier 1 Chinese mini-split OEM factories — Gree, Midea, Haier, Aux, and their OEM-only subsidiaries — routinely produce equipment rated at SEER2 18–22, well above the regional minimums. The compressor type is the primary determinant of where on that range a given unit lands.

**Japanese scroll compressors (Mitsubishi, Toshiba, Hitachi, Panasonic)** are used in the upper tier of Chinese OEM production. These are genuine OEM supply agreements, not counterfeit components — Mitsubishi Electric supplies compressors to several Chinese OEM factories under verified supply contracts. Units built around Japanese compressors reliably achieve their rated SEER2 and maintain efficiency across wide ambient temperature swings. The operating envelope for heating is typically -25°C to +24°C outdoor ambient, covering most US and European climate zones.
