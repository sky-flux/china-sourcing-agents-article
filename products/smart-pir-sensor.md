Smart PIR motion sensors are one of the highest-volume SKUs in the Zigbee and Z-Wave ecosystem. The commodity end of the market is crowded with sensors that meet spec on paper but fail in deployment — false triggers from HVAC drafts, battery life 40% below claimed, or firmware that refuses to join non-brand hubs. This page covers what separates a reliable OEM sensor from one that generates returns.

For sourcing and sampling of PIR sensor candidates, we evaluate factories across Shenzhen, Dongguan, and Guangzhou — most volume production sits in 8–12 factories that supply most of the branded smart home market under OEM agreements.

The passive infrared sensor comprises two subsystems: the pyroelectric element (the detector) and the Fresnel lens (the optical concentrator). Both affect false alarm rate, detection reliability, and pet immunity. They are independent components and must be evaluated separately.
