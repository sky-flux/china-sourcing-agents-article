SEER2 (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio 2) replaced SEER1 as the US energy efficiency standard for residential ductless heat pump equipment from January 2023. The US DOE revised the test methodology to use M1 test conditions — a higher static external pressure that more accurately reflects real-world ductless installation pressure drops — which results in SEER2 ratings roughly 5–7% lower than the SEER1 ratings previously assigned to the same HVAC equipment. Regional minimum requirements: SEER2 13.4 for split-system AC in the US Northern region, SEER2 15.2 in the Southern region. Inverter mini-splits are a major smart home appliance category, and Chinese OEM production is centered in the Foshan–Guangzhou HVAC corridor.

Tier 1 Chinese mini-split OEM factories — such as Gree, Midea, Haier, Aux, and their OEM-only subsidiaries — routinely produce energy-efficient cooling equipment rated at SEER2 18–22, well above the regional minimums. The compressor type is the primary determinant of where on that range a given wall-mounted AC unit lands.

**Japanese scroll compressors (Mitsubishi, Toshiba, Hitachi, Panasonic)** are used in the upper tier of Chinese OEM production. These are genuine OEM supply agreements, not counterfeit components. Units built around Japanese compressors reliably achieve their rated SEER2 and maintain efficiency across wide ambient temperature swings. The operating envelope for the heat pump is typically -25°C to +24°C outdoor ambient, covering most US and European climate zones.
