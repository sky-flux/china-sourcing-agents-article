The IEC 62040-3 standard defines Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) topology classes: VFI (Voltage and Frequency Independent), VI (Voltage Independent), and VFD (Voltage and Frequency Dependent). Online double-conversion is VFI-SS-111 — the highest protection class available. Our professional sourcing service helps enterprise buyers specify the exact correct topology for their application, because mismatching UPS topology to the specific load type is the single most common procurement mistake in wholesale UPS projects targeting industrial IoT, server rooms, and massive data center deployments.

**Online Double-Conversion (VFI).** All load power flows through the internal rectifier and inverter continuously: AC in → rectifier → DC bus → battery float charge → inverter → AC out. The critical load is always powered directly from the inverter, never directly from the utility grid input. The transfer time is 0ms (true zero) because there is no mechanical or static switching event — the inverter never stops running. Input power quality issues (voltage sags, surges, harmonic distortion, frequency drift) are completely isolated from the clean output. Output voltage and frequency are perfectly synthesized by the pure sine wave inverter independently of the dirty input grid.

**Line-Interactive (VI).** The inverter is in parallel with the output but only engages when utility input voltage goes significantly out of tolerance. Under normal conditions, AC utility power passes through a transformer or autotransformer with AVR (automatic voltage regulation) correction. When input drops below the safe threshold, a static transfer switch disconnects the utility and the battery inverter takes over. Transfer time: typically 4–8ms. For standard IT servers with ATX/EPS switch-mode power supplies, most easily tolerate an 8ms power gap without issue. However, for sensitive PLCs and process controllers executing real-time industrial control loops, a brief 8ms drop may cause a catastrophic watchdog reset.
