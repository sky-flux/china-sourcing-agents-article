When sourcing wholesale building blocks sets from a toy manufacturer in China, understanding international safety regulations is crucial. ASTM F963 (US Consumer Product Safety Improvement Act toy standard) and EN 71 (European toy safety standard) are largely aligned but not identical.

For wholesale buyers targeting both North American and European markets, testing to both standards is necessary. Combined testing adds approximately $800–1,200 per product, but it is a critical one-time cost to ensure compliance. Our audit service can verify a Chinese factory's existing test certifications and identify any compliance gaps before you pay for duplicate testing.

The small parts cylinder test determines choking hazard classification: any piece that fits entirely within a 31.7mm diameter × 57.1mm deep cylinder is classified as a small part. For custom plastic building bricks or sets labeled "3+ years", all individual interlocking pieces must be too large to fit the cylinder. Verify this during sample inspection — some 1×1 round studs on cheaper construction sets are borderline. Additionally, CA Prop 65 requires specific warning labels if any chemical in the product (including phthalates in ABS colorants) exceeds safe harbor thresholds.
