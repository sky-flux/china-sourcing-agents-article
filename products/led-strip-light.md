An LED strip light is a flexible circuit board populated with surface-mount LEDs, typically sold in 5-metre reels and powered by 12V or 24V DC. SMD2835 chips are used for general white-light applications; SMD5050 chips are used for RGB and higher-power tape. IP20 strips are for indoor dry locations, while IP65 (silicone sleeve) and IP68 (encapsulated) versions extend use to bathrooms, kitchens, outdoor signage, and marine environments. The low voltage and cuttable pitch make strips popular for architectural accent, under-cabinet, cove, and retail display lighting.

LED strip lights are one of the most counterfeited and spec-inflated product categories in China. When sourcing LED strips for smart home or architectural applications, key things to verify:

**Actual watt draw vs. rated.** Measure actual power consumption with a power meter on a sample reel. Budget strips frequently draw 60–70% of rated wattage because fewer LEDs are actually populated per meter or the current per LED is underdriven. Always verify electrical specs during quality inspection.
