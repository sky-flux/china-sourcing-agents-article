CE marking is the legal minimum for EU market entry, but GS (Geprüfte Sicherheit) certification is a voluntary mark tested by accredited bodies such as TÜV, DEKRA, or VDE. Major German DIY retailers (Bauhaus, Hornbach, OBI) and hardware distributors increasingly require GS as a buyer qualification — without it, you cannot get shelf space. Our audit and verification service can confirm whether a factory already holds a GS type certificate that can be extended to your branded version, or whether a new certification cycle is required. GS certification for angle grinders covers EN 60745-2-3 (specific to disc grinders) and requires factory audits plus periodic product re-testing. Budget 8–14 weeks for GS certification and $1,500–2,500 in testing fees.

Spindle lock mechanism durability is a common failure point on lower-tier units: the lock button should engage cleanly at zero RPM and withstand 10,000 lock/unlock cycles without play developing in the spindle bearing. Our inspection service can test spindle lock durability and guard retention on pre-production samples before you commit to a production run.

EU Directive 2002/44/EC (Physical Agents — Vibration) requires employers to monitor workers' daily vibration exposure. This means professional buyers increasingly demand accurate vibration emission values on the product declaration. The EN 60745-1 and EN 60745-2-3 test method specifies measurement conditions — ensure the factory provides a vibration test report from an accredited lab, not an in-house figure.
