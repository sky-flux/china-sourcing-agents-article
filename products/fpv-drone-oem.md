The video system is the single most commercially differentiated choice in an FPV drone OEM build. It determines certification path, receiver ecosystem compatibility, and your ability to private-label the finished product.

**Analog (5.8 GHz Raceband)** remains the dominant standard at the entry-to-mid price tier. The transmitter outputs on 40 standard Raceband channels (5658–5917 MHz) at switchable power levels — commonly 25 mW, 200 mW, 600 mW, and 1000 mW. Chinese factories producing analog VTX modules are numerous; the ecosystem around Fatshark, Skyzone, and generic diversity receivers is mature. For a white-label product, analog VTX modules are fully licensable — the factory can laser-engrave or PCB-print your brand with no IP gatekeeping. Pit mode (1–5 mW output for paddock use) is standard on any VTX above the entry tier; confirm it is selectable via OSD or Betaflight CLI before approving samples.

**DJI O3 (O3 Air Unit / O3 Pro)** delivers 1080p/60fps with latency around 30–40 ms, which is competitive for freestyle and cinematic use. The constraint is licensing: DJI does not license O3 IP for third-party OEM integration. Chinese factories can legally install O3 Air Unit modules as components sourced from DJI's distribution chain, but they cannot manufacture an O3-equivalent transmitter, and they cannot white-label the O3 Air Unit itself. What you receive is a drone *containing* a DJI-branded component — your private label covers the frame, FC, ESC, and motors, but the video link hardware carries DJI's mark. This is commercially viable for many buyers but is not a fully white-label digital video solution.
