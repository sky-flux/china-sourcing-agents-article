Bluetooth speaker factories cluster heavily around **Shenzhen (Longhua and Baoan districts)** and **Dongguan**. While most wholesale audio suppliers offer a vast catalog of reference designs with simple cosmetic customization (logo printing, custom colors), the real differentiation for premium brands lies in **firmware stability and acoustic tuning**. Our consumer electronics sourcing expertise helps you navigate this complex factory landscape to identify authentic audio manufacturers with genuine acoustic engineering teams, rather than just basic assembly lines. See our guide to sourcing electronics from China for a deeper dive.

**Acoustic tuning vs. spec sheet performance:** Frequency response graphs on factory datasheets are typically measured in anechoic chambers at 1 meter with the speaker driven at 1W. Real-world loudness, bass response, and distortion levels depend entirely on enclosure volume, passive radiator port tuning, and DSP equalization. We recommend requesting pink noise test files and comparing pre-production samples side by side at identical SPL (Sound Pressure Level) limits.

**Firmware customization scope:** Most custom Bluetooth speaker manufacturers run a shared firmware stack using Airoha, Jieli, or Qualcomm chipset SDKs. Custom button mapping, pairing behavior, EQ presets, and voice prompt replacement are standard NRE (Non-Recurring Engineering) items — expect $500–2,000 for basic customization. Because source code access is rarely granted, clarify upfront whether you will receive binary OTA (Over-The-Air) files or a private firmware branch.
