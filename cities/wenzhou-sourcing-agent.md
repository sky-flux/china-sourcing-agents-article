Wenzhou is the original proving ground for China's private enterprise economy — the so-called "Wenzhou Model" in which capital, manufacturing, and entrepreneurship built a dense cluster of specialist industry towns from the ground up, without state direction. Private enterprises still account for over 97% of the city's economic output. The practical consequence for buyers is extreme variety: within a 90-minute radius of Wenzhou city center you can source industrial valves, dress shoes, auto rubber parts, promotional bags, bathroom hardware, and low-voltage switchgear — each from a dedicated manufacturing district that has been refining its craft for 30–40 years.

Wenzhou holds the title of "city of valves" (阀门之城). The valve and pipe fitting manufacturing cluster — concentrated in Oubei Town, Yongjia County — is the largest in China by output volume. More than 1,800 valve enterprises operate here, producing butterfly valves, ball valves, gate valves, check valves, globe valves, and flanged pipe fittings in materials ranging from ductile iron and carbon steel to stainless 304/316 and special alloys.
