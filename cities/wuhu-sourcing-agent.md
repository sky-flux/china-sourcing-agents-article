---

Wuhu is the automotive capital of Anhui province and one of the most strategically located manufacturing cities on the Yangtze River. Its industrial character is defined above all by Chery Automobile, founded in Wuhu in 1997 and today one of China's largest vehicle exporters, shipping to more than 80 countries under the Chery, Exeed, Jaecoo, and Omoda brands. Chery's decades-long presence has seeded a deep tier-2 and tier-3 supplier ecosystem across the Wuhu Economic Development Zone and the broader Anhui province — one of the most significant automotive supply chains in central China.

For electronics and components buyers, Wuhu offers access to this automotive supply chain (particularly auto electronics, wiring harnesses, and PCB assemblies), a display component ecosystem tied to BOE Technology's local manufacturing plant, and the Yangtze River's logistics infrastructure for cost-effective heavy cargo movement to Shanghai and beyond.

Chery's headquarters and primary manufacturing campus are located in Wuhu's Economic and Technical Development Zone. The company's NEV (new energy vehicle) platforms — including the eQ pure electric series and the Arrizo hybrid platform — have driven significant investment in automotive electronics suppliers in and around the city. What was originally a conventional ICE automotive supply chain has evolved over the past decade into a cluster with genuine depth in EV-relevant hardware: motor controller PCB assemblies, battery management systems, vehicle CAN gateway modules, and automotive-grade power electronics sub-assemblies.
