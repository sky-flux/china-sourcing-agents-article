Two UL marks frequently appear on DIN rail power supplies, and they are absolutely not interchangeable. Our sourcing service helps you specify the correct UL standard for your industrial automation application — a critical distinction for industrial IoT and power electronics installations where control cabinet compliance is non-negotiable.

**UL 508 (Industrial Control Equipment).** This is the correct UL mark for switching power supplies installed inside industrial control panels, machine enclosures, and PLCs. UL 508 assumes the power supply is installed by a qualified electrician within a marked and enclosed panel. It does not require the same level of protection against accidental user contact as UL 60950. It is required for compliance with NFPA 79 (Electrical Standard for Industrial Machinery) in the North American market.

**UL 60950 / UL 62368-1 (IT Equipment / Audio/Video Equipment).** This standard is for power supplies used in IT equipment, consumer electronics, and AV products accessible to end users. It features different creepage and clearance requirements than UL 508. A UL 60950-listed power supply is not a substitute for UL 508 listing in a machine control panel — a certified inspector will reject it during commissioning.
