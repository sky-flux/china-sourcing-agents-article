The nominal sensing distance (Sn) printed on every inductive proximity sensor datasheet is measured against a mild steel target — specifically Fe360 (S235 equivalent), 1mm thick, square, with sides equal to the sensor's face diameter. If your application detects aluminum extrusions, stainless steel brackets, or brass fittings, the effective sensing range is substantially shorter.

These ranges reflect variation across sensor designs — coil geometry, oscillator frequency, and ferrite core construction all affect the correction factor. A well-designed M12 sensor with 4mm Sn detecting an aluminum target will sense at 1.6–2.0mm, not 4mm. If your machine design expects 3mm clearance to an aluminum target, that sensor will not reliably trigger.
