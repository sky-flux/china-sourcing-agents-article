---

DC barrel connectors are one of the most commoditized components in consumer electronics and power electronics products, and Chinese suppliers can deliver acceptable quality at very low cost for most applications. The failure modes are well-understood: contact spring degradation, plastic deformation under thermal stress, and plating wear in high-cycle applications. The design decision that matters most is selecting the right size and variant for your application upfront — mixing 5.5/2.1mm and 5.5/2.5mm in a product line costs more to resolve in the field than the entire connector BOM.

Barrel connectors provide a separable DC power connection consisting of a cylindrical outer barrel (negative in center-positive convention) and a center pin (positive). The connector is specified by two dimensions: outer barrel diameter and center pin diameter, both in millimeters. The mating engagement is a friction-fit sleeve; locking requires an accessory threaded collar or spring-loaded retention mechanism. Barrel connectors are not a high-reliability interface — they are designed for consumer electronics with moderate insertion cycle requirements (typically 500–5000 cycles depending on variant). For industrial or high-cycle applications above 2000 insertions, specify a locking variant or switch to M12 industrial circular connectors. Current ratings assume clean contacts at ambient 25°C. Derated by approximately 20% at 60°C ambient. For intermittent applications, thermal rise at the contact point is the limiting factor, not average current.
