# Alibaba vs 1688 vs Direct Factory: Which Should You Use?

> **Read the full guide with updates**: [china-sourcing-agents.com/blog/alibaba-vs-1688-vs-direct-factory](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/blog/alibaba-vs-1688-vs-direct-factory)

The three main sourcing channels for China electronics each have a specific role. Using the wrong one for where you are in your product lifecycle is one of the most common mistakes early hardware founders make.

---

## Alibaba

Alibaba (alibaba.com) is a B2B marketplace where Chinese suppliers list products for export. Most listings are in English. Trade Assurance provides payment protection.

**Good for:**
- Prototype and early sample orders
- Discovering product categories and understanding what exists
- Suppliers experienced with Western buyers (they've self-selected by creating English listings)
- Getting rough market pricing before negotiating

**Bad for:**
- Price — you're paying a 20–40% "English-speaking Western buyer" premium above factory gate
- Many listings are traders, not factories — middleman margin is embedded
- Niche components that aren't commonly exported

**The trader problem:** A trading company that sources from 5 different factories and marks up 40% looks identical on Alibaba to a manufacturer who makes the product themselves. Not all traders are bad (good ones add real value), but you're paying for it without knowing what you're getting.

---

## 1688

1688.com is the domestic Chinese version of Alibaba — for the Chinese market. Everything is in Chinese, priced in RMB, with no Trade Assurance for international buyers.

**Why it matters for foreign buyers:**
- Prices are dramatically lower — closer to what Chinese buyers actually pay
- More factories, fewer traders (there's no incentive to create English listings)
- Products that don't appear on Alibaba at all
- Real market pricing — lets you benchmark before Alibaba negotiations

**The friction:**
- Chinese only — every interaction needs translation
- No payment protection for overseas buyers
- MOQs designed for domestic wholesale, not international shipping
- You need a freight forwarder to ship internationally

**How to use it practically:**
1. Use it for price benchmarking before Alibaba negotiations
2. Find manufacturers on 1688, then contact directly on WeChat to discuss export terms
3. Use a [sourcing agent](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/services/sourcing) to handle communication and payment if you don't have Chinese-language capability

## Direct Factory

Direct factory means you've identified a specific manufacturer (through referrals, trade shows, or research) and deal with them outside any marketplace.

**What you get:**
- Best price — no platform commission, no trader margin
- Direct relationship with the people who make your product
- Easier customization (molds, certifications, packaging)
- Better leverage for quality issues

**The cost:**
- Finding factories is work — trade shows, referrals, cold outreach, on-site visits
- No platform protection — you're relying on relationship and due diligence
- Communication is harder without English-capable staff
- First orders require more verification work

**When to go direct:**
- You've identified a factory through a warm referral
- You know through sample orders which factory specifically makes your product
- You're at production volumes where savings justify the relationship investment
- You need significant customization that's easier without a middleman

## Quick comparison

| | Alibaba | 1688 | Direct Factory |
|---|---------|------|----------------|
| Language | English | Chinese only | Varies |
| Price vs. factory gate | +20–40% | Factory-level | Factory-level |
| Buyer protection | Trade Assurance | None for foreign buyers | Contract only |
| Trader ratio | ~40–60% | ~15–20% | 0% |
| Best use case | Samples, first contact | Price benchmarking | Production orders |

## The progression

Most hardware sourcing follows this path — not as a permanent choice, but as stages:

**Early → Alibaba**: Quick samples, test suppliers, understand the landscape. Pay the premium for convenience and protection.

**Validation → 1688 + comparison**: Cross-reference Alibaba pricing against 1688 to understand your leverage. Find factories that aren't on Alibaba.

**Production → Direct**: Once you've identified the right manufacturer, build a direct relationship for recurring orders. Staying on Alibaba for production volumes means paying platform fees and trader margins indefinitely.

---

## One rule for all three

Regardless of channel: **verify any supplier before placing a significant order.** A factory visit does more due diligence than any online verification. An Alibaba-verified supplier can still have a chaotic floor. See the [Factory Audit Checklist](factory-audit-checklist.md) for what to verify.
