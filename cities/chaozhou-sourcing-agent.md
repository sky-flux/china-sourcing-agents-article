Chaozhou is a city with a manufacturing identity defined almost entirely by two materials: premium ceramic and durable stainless steel. It holds roughly 50% of China's ceramic sanitary ware output, 25% of daily-use ceramics, and 30% of craft ceramics, earning formal designation as "China's Ceramics Capital." Simultaneously, Caitang Town within the city boundary is known as the "Steelware Capital," housing over 300 kitchenware factories in a highly vertically integrated cluster that runs seamlessly from raw stainless coil to finished cookware. This powerful combination — deep ceramic specialization and a parallel metal kitchenware cluster — makes Chaozhou one of China's absolute best procurement destinations for kitchen and bathroom product sourcing.

The city sits in eastern Guangdong, part of the bustling Chaozhou–Shantou–Jieyang metropolitan corridor. Jieyang Chaoshan International Airport (SWA) serves all three cities and handles both passenger and cargo traffic; Shantou Port, just 30 minutes by highway, provides essential ocean freight access to Southeast Asia, Japan, the Middle East, and onward to Europe and North America. The logistics infrastructure is fully proportionate to the massive export volume, as Chaozhou factories ship substantial annual tonnage through this global trade corridor. As a specialized **Chaozhou sourcing agent**, we offer complete supply chain management, stringent quality inspection, and reliable manufacturer verification.

**Ceramic sanitary ware** is the city's flagship B2B procurement category. Chaozhou's 1,200+ ceramic manufacturers supply toilets, basins, bathtubs, shower trays, and bidets to both domestic and international markets. Annual output is estimated at approximately 50 billion RMB. Factory scale ranges from small family workshops doing artisanal craft porcelain to massive mid-size export factories running 5–15 automated production lines with in-house glazing, high-temperature firing, and rigorous quality control. The city's immense depth in this category means buyers can find both OEM factories willing to produce to complex custom specifications and reliable white-label suppliers with established SKU catalogs and pre-existing international certifications. Certifications common among export-oriented factories include CE (EN 997, EN 14527), ASME A112.19.2, and ISO 9001.
