When sourcing surface mount capacitors, the dielectric class is the single most consequential specification on an MLCC (Multilayer Ceramic Capacitor). It determines temperature stability, capacitance change under applied DC voltage, and aging drift. Many BOM spec sheets underspecify this, leading to production surprises when a factory substitutes within the same nominal value for electronic components.

**C0G / NP0 (Class I — stable dielectric).** Capacitance change across the full operating temperature range: ±30ppm/°C maximum. Effectively zero DC bias effect. Zero piezoelectric noise. Ideal for timing circuits, oscillator load capacitors, RF matching networks, and any application where capacitance accuracy affects circuit function. Trade-off: C0G is only practical for values up to approximately 100nF in standard SMD case sizes — the ceramic formulation has lower permittivity than Class II materials. Price premium over X7R of the same nominal value is typically 2–4×.

**X7R (Class II — stable-ish).** Temperature coefficient: capacitance changes within ±15% of the nominal value across –55°C to +125°C. Additionally, X7R capacitors exhibit significant DC bias derating: at the rated voltage, capacitance can drop 30–60% below the nominal value. X7R is the standard choice for bypass and decoupling capacitors, coupling capacitors, and filtering where ±15% variation is acceptable. At 0402 and 0603 case sizes, X7R is the most commercially available and competitively priced dielectric class from Chinese MLCC manufacturers.
