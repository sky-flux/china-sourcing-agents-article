Quanzhou is one of China's most productive manufacturing cities by output value, and yet it remains underrepresented in international sourcing conversations dominated by Shenzhen, Guangzhou, and Yiwu. The reason is straightforward: Quanzhou's output is physical goods — shoes, sportswear, bathroom fittings, baby products — that move through trade channels rather than exhibitor-heavy Canton Fair pavilions. For buyers in these product categories, Quanzhou represents some of the deepest manufacturing infrastructure in China, anchored by the Jinjiang district's globally significant footwear cluster and supported by one of the country's most concentrated pools of listed consumer goods companies.

Jinjiang district, administered within Quanzhou prefecture, is formally designated China's "Shoe Capital" — not as a promotional title but as an industrial classification reflecting actual output. Jinjiang's 2,300+ footwear manufacturers account for approximately 40% of China's total footwear production by volume and roughly 20% of global footwear output. Sports and travel shoes are the dominant categories, with athletic footwear representing the largest segment.

The district's international profile is anchored by Anta (安踏), Xtep (特步), 361°, and ERKE — all headquartered in Jinjiang, all publicly listed, and all operating global supply chains from this base. Anta alone has exceeded $6 billion in annual revenue and holds rights to FILA China, Descente, and Arcteryx, which gives Jinjiang's manufacturing infrastructure a direct connection to globally recognized performance brands.
