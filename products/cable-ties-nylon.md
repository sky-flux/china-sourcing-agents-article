PA66 (Nylon 6,6) has a higher melting point (265°C vs 220°C for PA6), better resistance to heat aging, and superior dimensional stability. For electrical enclosures and automotive wire harnesses where operating temperatures exceed 80°C, PA66 is the correct specification. Our sourcing service identifies factories that provide material traceability and third-party DSC test reports to verify PA66 grade, preventing the common PA6 substitution problem.

Verify material using a melting point test or DSC (differential scanning calorimetry) — PA66 has a distinctive melting peak at 265°C vs 220°C for PA6. A simpler field test: PA66 will not ignite easily when held to an open flame and self-extinguishes quickly; PA6 burns more readily. For outdoor use, UV stabilizer content is critical: black cable ties use 2% carbon black as both UV absorber and colorant — natural white UV-stabilized ties require 0.3–0.5% UV-8 (or equivalent HALS-type) additive, which is invisible and easily omitted by cost-cutting suppliers.

UL 94V-2 means the material self-extinguishes within 30 seconds after removal of a flame, and burning drips are allowed (V-0 does not allow burning drips). For cable management in electrical panels and switchgear, UL 94V-2 is the minimum acceptable rating — some switchgear manufacturers require V-0. Verify the UL 94 rating from the resin supplier's datasheet and ensure the factory uses the same qualified resin lot. Our audit service can verify resin supplier documentation and UL 94 certification during factory visits.
