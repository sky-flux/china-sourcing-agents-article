An nRF52840 or nRF52833 BLE module is a pre-certified Bluetooth 5.x radio subsystem that combines the Nordic chip, crystals, matching network, and antenna into a surface-mount package. It is used to add Bluetooth Low Energy connectivity to wearables, health devices, and IoT sensors without designing RF circuits from scratch. Most modules arrive pre-programmed with the Nordic SoftDevice and connect to a host MCU or run application code directly on the module's integrated core.

Any product using the word "Bluetooth" in marketing materials — including product names, packaging, and listings — must be qualified through the Bluetooth Special Interest Group (Bluetooth SIG) Qualified Design ID (QDID) program. This is a licensing requirement, not a regulatory certification. For more on certification requirements for wireless modules when working with a **BLE module manufacturer in China**, see our guide to CE and FCC certification for electronics.

1. **Use a pre-qualified module.** If the module manufacturer holds a valid QDID for the module (covering the BT Host, Controller, and Profile layers relevant to your use case), you can list your end product using the SIG's product listing process with a reduced qualification fee (~$10,000 cheaper than full testing).
