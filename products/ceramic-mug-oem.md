An OEM custom ceramic mug is a blank or decorated drinking vessel produced to a buyer's artwork, glaze specification, and packaging. It is one of the most common promotional products for coffee brands, corporate gifting programs, and subscription-box merchants because the unit cost is low, decoration options are flexible, and the product ships flat in standard sea cartons. Buyers typically source stoneware for everyday price-point lines and porcelain for premium, thinner-wall designs.

The three dominant print methods for custom ceramic mugs and bulk printed coffee mugs differ in durability, cost, and regulatory footprint. Our private label service helps you evaluate the right print method for your promotional drinkware price point and volume, and manages the entire branding process from artwork approval to production sampling.

**Sublimation** is the most common for photo-quality and full-wrap designs on promotional logo mugs. The dye is transferred onto a polymer coating (usually polyester-based) applied over the glaze surface. Because the dye never penetrates the ceramic or glaze body, it is not directly food-contact — the underlying glaze is. However, sublimation coatings can degrade after 500+ dishwasher cycles, causing the image to fade or the coating to flake. When specifying sublimation mugs, request a dishwasher durability test per ASTM C1027 (decorative resistance to dishwasher damage). Firing temperature in sublimation processes is low (180–210°C in the transfer press), so the ceramic itself undergoes no additional glaze-sealing step.
