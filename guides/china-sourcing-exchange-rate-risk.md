# Exchange Rate Risk in China Sourcing: Protect Your Budget

> **Read the full guide**: [https://china-sourcing-agents.com/guides/china-sourcing-exchange-rate-risk/](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/guides/china-sourcing-exchange-rate-risk/)

When your currency weakens against USD or RMB, your landed cost from China rises fast. How electronics buyers manage FX risk without a treasury team.

---

Currency movement is a hidden cost that most consumer electronics buyers ignore until it hits them. A 10% swing in your local currency against the USD can wipe out your entire margin on an order — without anything going wrong at the factory. Here is how to account for FX exposure and what you can actually do about it, including when it makes sense to talk to a sourcing consultant before locking a large order.

Nearly all Chinese factory quotes are denominated in USD. That looks safe if you are a USD buyer, but it is only half the picture. Factory costs — labor, components, energy — are paid in RMB. When RMB appreciates against USD, factories absorb the squeeze initially, then reprice at renewal. A 10% RMB appreciation in 2023–2024 eroded margins by 6–8% for buyers who locked in USD prices but had not built headroom into their landed cost models.

For EUR, GBP, AUD, or JPY buyers, the exposure is more direct. You quote your customer in local currency, pay the factory in USD, and any move in the USD/EUR or USD/GBP rate during the order cycle comes straight off your bottom line. An order that penciled out at 30% gross margin can close at 22% if your currency weakens 8% between deposit and final payment — which is a realistic move over a 90-day production window.
