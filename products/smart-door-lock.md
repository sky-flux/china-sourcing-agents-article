Smart locks are both a security product and an IoT device — they have mechanical failure modes, firmware vulnerabilities, and regulatory requirements for each target market. When sourcing smart home devices from China, these dual concerns require parallel due diligence.

**Fingerprint sensor technology.** Capacitive sensors (required) vs. optical sensors: optical sensors can be fooled by a high-quality photograph of a fingerprint. For any application where security matters, insist on capacitive semiconductor sensors. Verify the sensor module brand (IDEX, Goodix, or equivalent). Test fingerprint recognition performance across a range of skin conditions: wet hands, dirty hands, cold hands (vasoconstriction changes fingerprint contact quality). Acceptable false rejection rate (FRR) is below 1%.

**ANSI/BHMA Grade 2 or Grade 1.** For the US market, commercial buyers and rental property managers expect ANSI/BHMA Grade 2 (residential) or Grade 1 (commercial) ratings — these test cycle life (250,000 vs. 500,000 cycles), bolt strength, and forced entry resistance. A factory audit should verify ANSI/BHMA certification from an accredited lab. Grade 1 certification from an accredited lab is required for most commercial applications.
