Light quantity for optimal plant growth is measured in photosynthetically active radiation (PAR) — photons in the 400–700nm wavelength range that drive photosynthesis. The three critical metrics that matter for specifying a commercial LED grow light are PPFD, DLI, and photon efficacy.

**PPFD (Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density), μmol/m²/s.** The instantaneous photon flux density at a specific point, measured at a specific distance from the fixture. The relevant value for indoor farming grow light specification is the average PPFD across the entire canopy footprint (not merely the peak center value, which some Chinese factories misleadingly report). A 600W fixture at 600mm hanging height delivering 1,200 μmol/m²/s at center and 500 μmol/m²/s at corners has a very different agronomic performance than a fixture averaging 900 μmol/m²/s uniformly across the same footprint. Always request a full PPFD map (grid measurement at 150mm spacing) from an independent photometric test lab, not the factory's own unverified measurement.

**DLI (Daily Light Integral), mol/m²/day.** The total photon dose delivered per day = PPFD × photoperiod hours × 3,600 / 1,000,000. For indoor cultivation with an 18h photoperiod at 600 μmol/m²/s: DLI = 600 × 18 × 3600 / 1,000,000 = 38.9 mol/m²/day. This is the definitive metric that crop scientists use to correlate light to crop yield. Tomatoes require a DLI of 20–40 mol/m²/day for commercial yield; leafy greens need 12–17 mol/m²/day. Specify the target DLI for your specific crop before calculating fixture power and physical spacing.
