The dielectric class is the single most consequential specification on an MLCC. It determines temperature stability, capacitance change under applied DC voltage, and aging drift. Many BOM spec sheets underspecify this, leading to production surprises when a factory substitutes within the same nominal value.

**C0G / NP0 (Class I — stable dielectric).** Capacitance change across the full operating temperature range: ±30ppm/°C maximum. Effectively zero DC bias effect. Zero piezoelectric noise. Ideal for timing circuits, oscillator load capacitors, RF matching networks, and any application where capacitance accuracy affects circuit function. Trade-off: C0G is only practical for values up to approximately 100nF in standard case sizes — the ceramic formulation has lower permittivity than Class II materials, so higher values require physically larger cases or are simply unavailable. Price premium over X7R of the same nominal value is typically 2–4×.

**X7R (Class II — stable-ish).** Temperature coefficient: capacitance changes within ±15% of the nominal value across –55°C to +125°C. Additionally, X7R capacitors exhibit significant DC bias derating: at the rated voltage, capacitance can drop 30–60% below the nominal value depending on manufacturer and case size. For decoupling applications where a 10µF capacitor might actually deliver 4–6µF under operating bias, this is a real design constraint, not a datasheet footnote. X7R is the standard choice for bypass and decoupling capacitors, coupling capacitors, and filtering where ±15% variation is acceptable. At 0402 and 0603 case sizes, X7R is the most commercially available and competitively priced dielectric class from Chinese manufacturers.
