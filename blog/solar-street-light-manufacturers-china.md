Search for "solar street light manufacturers in China" and the results split into two very different supply bases: fixture specialists around Zhongshan and Guzhen who excel at LED housings and optics, and system integrators around Yangzhou and Jiangsu who assemble complete solar street lights for municipal and export projects. Knowing which cluster a factory belongs to tells you a lot about what it actually does well.

Most buyers landing on this page are municipal contractors, Africa or Middle East distributors, or procurement teams running government tenders. They are not looking for a single garden lamp; they need hundreds or thousands of units with consistent battery chemistry, honest panel ratings, and paperwork that clears customs. The real risk in this category is not the LED — it is the battery. The most common fraud we see is a supplier claiming a LiFePO4 battery while shipping ternary lithium or recycled cells. That mislabeling affects cycle life, fire safety, and whether the shipment can even be air-freighted without a proper UN 38.3 report.

This list covers ten manufacturers that are genuinely active in solar street lighting. I have sorted them by what they do best, not by who pays the most for marketing. The price band across the category sits at roughly $25–$120 per unit depending on wattage and battery configuration, and the two dominant production clusters are Zhongshan/Guzhen in Guangdong for fixtures and Jiangsu/Yangzhou for complete solar street light systems.
