When selecting an electric golf cart manufacturer in China, a 48V 100Ah LiFePO4 pack generally costs approximately $400–600 more upfront than an equivalent VRLA lead-acid pack. However, for wholesale electric golf carts, the economics quickly flip over a 3-year period: LiFePO4 delivers 2,000–3,000 charge cycles to 80% capacity vs just 400–600 for lead-acid. Furthermore, LiFePO4 maintains consistent performance across the entire discharge curve. Lead-acid carts require monthly water top-up, terminal cleaning, and equalization charging — labor costs that add up in a large fleet. LiFePO4 is maintenance-free. For resort and hotel applications where maintenance staff availability is limited, LiFePO4 is the practical choice despite higher initial cost. Verify the BMS includes cell balancing: without balancing, LiFePO4 cells drift in capacity over time and one weak cell limits the entire pack.

For road-legal operation of electric golf buggies on public roads in the US (including resort paths that cross public roads), vehicles must rigorously comply with FMVSS 500 (Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard for Low-Speed Vehicles). This covers crucial components like headlights, taillights, turn signals, mirrors, and windshields. DOT certification is required for these low-speed vehicles targeting maximum speeds of 40–45 km/h. While wholesale electric golf carts operating strictly on private property (golf courses, warehouse campuses) do not legally require DOT certification, many enterprise fleet managers still demand it for insurance and liability purposes when purchasing from a golf cart supplier.
