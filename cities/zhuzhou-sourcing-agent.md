Zhuzhou (株洲) is a mid-sized industrial city in Hunan province that deserves far more attention from international sourcing professionals than it receives. Forty minutes south of Changsha by high-speed rail, it sits at the eastern corner of the Chang-Zhu-Tan metropolitan cluster — an agglomeration of three cities that together form Hunan's industrial core. Two industries define Zhuzhou's global significance: CRRC's rail traction electronics and IGBT power semiconductor manufacturing, and China's largest tungsten carbide tooling production base. A third — non-ferrous metal smelting, including world-leading bismuth, indium, and germanium output — completes a picture of a city punching well above its population weight in global supply chains.

CRRC Zhuzhou Electric Locomotive (中车株洲电力机车有限公司) and CRRC Zhuzhou Institute (中车株洲电力机车研究所, operating commercially as CRRC Times Electric, 中车时代电气) are the two pillars of Zhuzhou's electronics reputation. Together they constitute China's most advanced traction electronics capability and one of the world's leading IGBT manufacturing operations outside Europe and Japan.

CRRC Times Electric (listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange as 3898.HK) manufactures the power electronics systems that make China's high-speed rail network operate. Every CRH series and CR400 series high-speed train runs on traction inverters and motor control systems developed and manufactured in Zhuzhou. The power electronics IGBT modules at the heart of those inverters — insulated-gate bipolar transistors that switch the high-voltage DC from overhead catenary into the variable-frequency AC that drives traction motors — are produced at CRRC Times' Zhuzhou semiconductor facility at voltage classes ranging from 1,200 V to 6,500 V.
