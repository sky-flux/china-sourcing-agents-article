Changchun is China's Automobile City — a title that reflects a manufacturing identity defined over 70 years by FAW (First Auto Works, 第一汽车集团), the country's oldest state-owned automaker, founded here in 1953. The FAW industrial complex alone covers more than 40 square kilometers in Changchun's Kuancheng district. Layered on top of the automotive base are two other nationally significant industries: CRRC Changchun Railway Vehicles, one of the world's largest manufacturers of high-speed train cars and metro vehicles, and the Changchun Institute of Optics, Fine Mechanics and Physics (CIOMP), which anchors a precision optics and machine vision manufacturing cluster that most buyers outside China have never considered.

For international buyers, Changchun offers access to an automotive supply chain shaped by German and Japanese OEM quality discipline, railway electronics certified to European EN standards, and precision optical instruments at prices that undercut Western alternatives by a wide margin.
