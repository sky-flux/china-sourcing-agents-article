---

Chengdu is the capital of Sichuan province, the largest city in Southwest China by GDP, and one of the few inland Chinese cities with a diversified manufacturing base — spanning consumer electronics, automotive, industrial IoT, food processing, and a growing medical device sector. For procurement teams, Chengdu matters as the main electronics hub of Western China and as the starting point for the China–Europe rail freight corridor, which changes the logistics and supply chain calculus for EU buyers. A **Chengdu sourcing agent** helps buyers navigate this ecosystem while handling quality control and manufacturer verification.

The anchor tenants in Chengdu's electronics manufacturing base are household names: Intel operates a chip assembly and test facility here; Foxconn's Chengdu campus historically produced iPad assemblies and continues contract electronics manufacturing; BOE runs AMOLED display panel production; and Dell runs a China design and assembly center in the Tianfu New Area.

These primary facilities are not directly accessible to independent international buyers — their capacity is committed to OEM contracts — but their value to B2B procurement lies in what they created around them: a 12,000+ manufacturer ecosystem calibrated to tier-1 OEM quality expectations. Foxconn's supply chain requirements pushed nearby suppliers to implement AQL inspection, FMEA documentation, and process control systems that most Pearl River Delta SME suppliers adopted only later, or not at all.
