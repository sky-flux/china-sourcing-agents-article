A 12V car air purifier is a compact cabin device that pulls air through a HEPA filter and activated-carbon layer, often supplemented by a negative-ion generator, to reduce PM2.5, VOCs, and odors. It is sold as an OEM accessory, private-label consumer product, or fleet add-on for markets where in-cabin air quality is a purchasing consideration.

Wholesale car air purifiers and portable auto air cleaners are a high-competition, marketing-heavy category. The cabin volume of a typical car (2–3m³) means even a modest CADR of 15 m³/h delivers effective air changes, but ozone generation from ionizers is a genuine safety concern. For more on sourcing automotive electronics from China, including certification requirements specific to in-vehicle devices, see our automotive industry page. Guangzhou and Shenzhen host many factories that assemble these units, making on-site inspection practical once samples pass chamber testing.

**Ozone generation limit.** Some portable car ionizers produce ozone as a byproduct. The California Air Resources Board (CARB) limits indoor ozone from air cleaning devices to <0.050 ppm. The WHO indoor air quality guideline is even lower. Specify "no detectable ozone" or "<0.01 ppm ozone generation" and require an accredited lab measurement — this is a legal requirement for California sales and a genuine health concern for in-vehicle use where ventilation is limited.
