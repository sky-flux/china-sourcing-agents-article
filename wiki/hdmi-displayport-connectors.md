# HDMI & DisplayPort Connectors: China Sourcing Reference

HDMI and DisplayPort connectors from China: HDMI licensing, version bandwidth, royalty-free DP advantage, impedance specs, and high-speed failure modes.

---

HDMI and DisplayPort are the two dominant video interfaces in consumer and professional display products, and they represent opposite sourcing risk profiles. HDMI carries mandatory licensing fees and product certification requirements from the HDMI Forum — products shipping without proper licensing infringe IP and face import seizure. DisplayPort is royalty-free under VESA. Both interfaces have strict contact impedance and plating requirements that commodity Chinese connectors frequently fail to meet, causing signal integrity failures that are not detectable at incoming inspection without specialized test equipment.

**HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface)** is a proprietary standard controlled by the HDMI Forum (hdmi.org). To manufacture or sell products with HDMI, companies must join the HDMI Forum as adopters ($5,000–$15,000 annual fee depending on revenue tier) and complete per-product compliance testing at an authorized test lab. The HDMI trademark and logo cannot legally appear on products that have not completed this process. Chinese manufacturers producing uncertified "HDMI" connectors and products are producing IP-infringing goods — importing them creates legal risk in the US, EU, and Japan markets. HDMI and DisplayPort connectors are central to consumer electronics sourcing, from TV boxes to mini projectors; working with a sourcing agent who understands compliance overhead prevents costly import detentions, and inspection at production verifies contact plating and shielding continuity before shipment.

**DisplayPort (DP)** is a VESA standard with no royalty fees and no mandatory product certification. VESA Compliance Testing is available but optional. This makes DisplayPort fundamentally easier to source: a connector that meets the physical and electrical specifications is a compliant DisplayPort connector, with no IP licensing overhead.
