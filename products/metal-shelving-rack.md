A heavy-duty metal shelving rack is a boltless steel storage system built from upright posts, horizontal beams, and removable shelves. It is used in warehouses, workshops, retail backrooms, and light-industrial facilities where pallet racking is overkill but floor stacking wastes space. These racks are especially common in consumer-electronics warehouses, where small cartons and spare parts need dense, accessible storage. Most units ship flat-packed; a 20GP container typically holds 80–110 five-tier sets depending on packaging density, which keeps sea freight per unit under control.

When ordering wholesale heavy-duty metal shelving racks, UDL (uniformly distributed load) is the standard for shelf load capacity — it assumes load is evenly spread across the entire shelf surface. Point load capacity is always lower: a 350 kg UDL shelf might only handle 120 kg as a point load at the centre. For warehouse buyers and retail distributors stacking palletized goods, point load behaviour matters. Ask the factory for both figures and verify against your actual storage scenario during sourcing and supplier evaluation.

Steel gauge is frequently misrepresented. 18-gauge steel is 1.2mm thick — many factories supply 20-gauge (0.9mm) uprights while quoting 18-gauge. Bring calipers to the sample inspection and measure upright wall thickness at multiple points — this is what a factory audit should verify on the production line. The boltless connector (rivet-in lug) is the structural weak point: verify connector engagement depth and pull-out force. A simple pull-out test with a luggage scale should show >200 kg pull-out resistance per connector.
