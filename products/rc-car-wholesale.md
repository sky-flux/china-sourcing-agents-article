A 1:10 scale brushless 4WD RC off-road car is a radio-controlled vehicle built on a four-wheel-drive chassis with independent suspension, oil-filled coilover shocks, and a brushless motor paired with an electronic speed controller (ESC). These models are sold B2B in carton quantities—usually 12 to 24 units per master carton—and are positioned either as toys for children under 14 or as entry-level hobby units for teens and adults. The positioning is not cosmetic: toy-grade cars must meet EN 71 and ASTM F963 safety standards, while hobby-grade cars prioritize replaceable parts and higher speeds but still require FCC Part 15 authorization for the 2.4GHz transmitter. This places RC cars at the intersection of the toy and consumer electronics supply chains.

Before placing a wholesale order, lock down the following details in writing:

The most expensive mistake we see is a factory dropping a 4,000 KV motor into a chassis geared for 3,300 KV. The result is overheating, ESC shutdown, and a return rate that can exceed 8% on Amazon. Another frequent issue is outdated or borrowed certificates: a supplier may show you an EN 71 report that covers a previous radio module or a different paint lot. Always cross-check the report model number, test date, and lab accreditation against the actual sample.
