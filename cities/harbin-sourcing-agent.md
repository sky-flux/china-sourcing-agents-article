Harbin is the capital of Heilongjiang Province and the northernmost major manufacturing city in China. Its industrial identity was defined by Soviet-assisted industrialization in the 1950s: Harbin Electric Group became one of the world's largest manufacturers of power generation equipment, supplying hydro turbines, steam turbines, and large generators to power plants across Asia, Africa, and Latin America.

This history created a manufacturing base that is uniquely engineering-intensive by Chinese standards — deep in heavy electrical and mechanical production, with a secondary supply chain of precision electrical components, industrial control electronics, and ruggedized hardware built around serving the power industry.
