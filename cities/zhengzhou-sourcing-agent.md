---

Zhengzhou is the capital of Henan, China's most populous province, and it punches significantly above its weight in global manufacturing and logistics. The combination of the world's largest iPhone assembly campus, a major cross-border e-commerce aviation hub, China's geographic center for rail freight, and Henan's enormous industrial output in food processing and automotive components makes Zhengzhou one of the most strategically important inland manufacturing cities in China.

Most overseas buyers think of Zhengzhou primarily in terms of Foxconn. That association is accurate but incomplete. The city's role as a supply chain hub extends well beyond consumer electronics into automotive, food processing equipment, and aluminum — and its logistics infrastructure makes it a serious rival to coastal cities for air and rail freight efficiency.

Foxconn's Zhengzhou campus in the Airport Economic Zone is the world's largest iPhone assembly facility. At peak production (typically October–December), the campus employs around 300,000 workers across a facility occupying several square kilometers. The campus produces a large share of global iPhone shipments annually.
