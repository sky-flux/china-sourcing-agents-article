Smart downlights are the highest-volume fixture in a Gulf villa or apartment fit-out, and the single most common Saudi rejection point is efficiency: SASO 2902:2023 requires **≥90 lm/W**, mandatory since 2025-06-01. A common 80 lm/W RGBCW downlight that ships fine elsewhere will be rejected at SABER. This page covers what to verify on an RGBCW smart downlight sourced from China for the Saudi and GCC market.

A smart downlight combines an LED engine, a constant-current driver, and a wireless control board. The efficacy figure is measured across the *whole fixture* at the rated drive current — not the bare LED. Two pitfalls:

The action item: require an **accredited photometric report** (ILAC lab) showing ≥90 lm/W at the production current, with the model number matching the unit. This is cheap to verify before order and expensive to discover at customs.
