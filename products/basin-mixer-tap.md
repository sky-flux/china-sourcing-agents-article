A basin mixer tap is a single-lever faucet that blends hot and cold water at the spout, controlling both flow and temperature with one handle. It is the standard fixture for bathroom vanities in Europe, the UK, and most residential markets, and is sold as an OEM or private-label item by plumbing merchants, Amazon sellers, and hotel developers. Because it sits in direct contact with potable water, every market regulates the brass alloy, lead content, and cartridge performance before sale — which is why buyers often treat basin taps as part of the broader home-improvement supply chain.

Selling basin taps across multiple European markets requires market-specific certifications — there is no single pan-European water contact certification. WRAS covers the UK; ACS (Attestation de Conformité Sanitaire) is required in France; DVGW/KTW (Deutsches Institut für Normung) covers Germany; and KIWA/WKB covers the Netherlands.
