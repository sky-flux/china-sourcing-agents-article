Putian (莆田) is best understood not as a single-industry city but as an industrial cluster city where an unusual combination of sectors has grown in relative geographic isolation from China's better-known manufacturing hubs. It is China's second-largest footwear manufacturing city after the Quanzhou-Jinjiang corridor, responsible for producing athletic and casual shoes that have shipped under major international brand labels through both authorized OEM channels and, historically, through unauthorized replica networks.

The distinction between these two channels is auditable and matters enormously for buyers. Beyond footwear, Putian also operates a meaningful medical consumables cluster and produces granite and stone products that export to construction markets globally. For most international buyers, Putian is not on the sourcing radar until they encounter a footwear factory audit recommendation, a medical supply tender, or a granite specification. That limited visibility works in buyers' favor: factories here compete harder for legitimate international orders than comparable factories in better-promoted clusters. However, safely navigating this terrain requires a professional **Putian sourcing agent** to conduct factory audits, enforce strict quality control, and manage procurement logistics.
