The smart home lighting module ecosystem has consolidated around a few chipsets — Silicon Labs EFR32, Nordic nRF52840, and Texas Instruments CC2652. Which chipset matters because it determines firmware update frequency and long-term support. When evaluating factories, verify that the actual production PCB uses the chipset stated in the datasheet — substitution to a cheaper Beken or Realtek module is common in this price segment.

**Matter certification.** Matter-certified products must have a CSA-issued device attestation certificate (DAC) burned into the device at the factory. Ask the factory for their CSA vendor ID and verify it in the Matter Product Database. Non-certified products marketed as "Matter compatible" will fail Apple HomeKit setup. See our Matter certification overview for details on the certification process.

**Zigbee coordinator compatibility testing.** The Zigbee Alliance certification does not guarantee interoperability with all coordinators. Test sample units against your target hub (Philips Hue Bridge, Amazon Echo, SmartThings) before committing to mass production. Budget 2–3 weeks for compatibility testing across at least 4 coordinator brands.
