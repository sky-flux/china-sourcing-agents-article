A wholesale building blocks set is a construction toy made from injection-molded ABS plastic bricks, usually sold in 500-, 750-, or 1,000-piece cartons. The sets are designed to be LEGO-compatible—meaning the 9.6 mm stud pitch, wall thickness, and clutch geometry match the mainstream system—and are marketed to children aged 3 and up. Most B2B orders are custom-branded versions of existing molds, with packaging, instructions, and sometimes a small number of unique graphic pieces. The category sits within the broader consumer electronics and plastics manufacturing ecosystem around Shantou and Shenzhen.

The two biggest quality failures in building blocks are recycled ABS sold as virgin resin and inconsistent stud clutch force. Recycled resin can deform after a few assembly cycles and may fail phthalate migration limits. Clutch force below 2 N produces bricks that fall apart during play; above 8 N, children cannot separate them. Both issues are only detectable through sample testing, not by looking at photos.
