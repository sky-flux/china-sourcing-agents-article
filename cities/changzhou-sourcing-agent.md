Changzhou has risen to become one of China's most strategically positioned new energy vehicle manufacturing cities — ranked among the country's top four NEV production bases with over 800,000 vehicles produced annually and a cluster output exceeding 500 billion RMB in 2025. The city's NEV ecosystem spans more than 3,000 enterprises covering the full supply chain from powertrain and braking systems to body components, charging infrastructure, and lighting. Located between Nanjing (30 minutes by HSR) and Shanghai (1.5 hours), Changzhou offers Yangtze River Delta logistics advantages alongside one of China's most complete automotive component supply chains.

Beyond vehicles, Changzhou has established clusters in LED lighting, electric two-wheelers, and compact machinery — making it a multi-category manufacturing destination for buyers with diversified sourcing needs.

**NEV and auto parts** represent Changzhou's most distinctive and deepest capability for automotive electronics buyers. The 3,000+ enterprises in the NEV cluster cover every layer of the supply chain: powertrain components (motors, inverters, reducers), braking and steering systems, body structural components, thermal management assemblies, and interior electronics. For international buyers, the most accessible entry points are tier-2 and tier-3 suppliers who produce to automotive-grade quality standards — IATF 16949 processes, ASIL functional safety documentation, and incoming inspection requirements calibrated to major OEM standards. Custom auto parts from Changzhou typically require 8–14 weeks for first-time orders with tooling; standard platform components are available in 4–6 weeks.
