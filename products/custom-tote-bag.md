A custom tote bag is a reusable carry bag manufactured to a buyer’s specification: fabric weight, handle length, print artwork, color, and packaging. Cotton canvas, non-woven polypropylene (PP), and recycled PET (RPET) are the three most common materials, ordered in quantities from 500 pieces for small corporate events up to tens of thousands for retail or supermarket chains. Because the product is low-cost per unit, buyers often treat it as an afterthought, but quality failures — torn handles, misaligned prints, or chemical odors — turn a brand touchpoint into a liability. The most successful sourcing runs treat the tote bag as a packaging and compliance product, not just a commodity print job.

When sourcing from a **custom tote bag manufacturer in China**, OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certifies that every component of a textile product (fabric, thread, printing inks, zippers, buttons) has been tested for harmful substances and is harmless to human health. For reusable shopping bags, custom printed bags for baby items, or items given as gifts to families with children, skin contact is continuous — making OEKO-TEX a credible third-party safety claim. Major European retailers (Aldi, Lidl, Carrefour) actively include OEKO-TEX as a strict purchasing requirement for promotional textile items and corporate gifting. Our sourcing service identifies factories with valid OEKO-TEX certification that covers all components — not just the base fabric — and our factory audit service verifies the certification scope before you commit your budget.

The certification requires all components to be tested — not just the base fabric. Screen printing inks are a common failure point for many wholesale canvas bags: azo dyes that can release carcinogenic aromatic amines are banned under OEKO-TEX and EU REACH (EN ISO 14362-1). Always request OEKO-TEX certification documents that list the factory name, product description, and all components tested. A certificate covering only the fabric does not cover the inks, meaning your eco-friendly promotional products might still fail compliance checks.
