# M8 & M16/M23 Circular Connectors: China Sourcing Reference

> **Read the full guide**: [https://china-sourcing-agents.com/wiki/m8-m16-circular-connectors/](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/wiki/m8-m16-circular-connectors/)

M8, M16, and M23 circular connectors from China: IEC 61076-2-104 specs, servo motor and encoder wiring, contact resistance, and counterfeit detection.

---

M8, M16, and M23 designate circular connectors by shell thread diameter (8mm, 16mm, 23mm). Each format occupies a distinct application niche: M8 for space-constrained sensors, M16 for general-purpose multi-pin field wiring, and M23 as the accepted standard for servo motor encoder and power connections in European industrial IoT automation — such as servo drives and industrial gateways. All three are sourced from China at scale, but M23 carries the highest counterfeit risk due to its use in precision motion control systems where contact failure produces motor drive faults that are expensive to diagnose.

The M8 standard is defined by IEC 61076-2-104. M16 has no single governing IEC standard (manufacturers follow DIN 45323 or proprietary dimensional specifications), while M23 is governed by IEC 61076-2-107. All three share the screw-lock thread convention and circular contact arrangement. IP67 is the baseline sealing requirement for field-deployed variants; IP68 is available from selected manufacturers. Operating temperature for standard-grade units is −25°C to +85°C; industrial grade extends to −40°C.

3-pin M8 connectors carry +V, 0V, and signal output — used with 2-wire and 3-wire PNP/NPN proximity sensors. 4-pin adds a second signal pin or alarm output (common on photoelectric sensors). 6-pin M8 exists for combined power + 2-channel output (e.g., dual-output distance sensors) but is less common. The key dimensional constraint is the 8mm shell, which requires 5mm× diameter cable — M8 connectors with M12 cables are a common sourcing error.
