# FPC & FFC Connectors: China Sourcing Reference

> **Read the full guide**: [https://china-sourcing-agents.com/wiki/fpc-ffc-connectors/](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/wiki/fpc-ffc-connectors/)

FPC vs FFC connectors from China: 0.5–1.25mm pitch, ZIF vs LIF lock types, actuation cycle life, camera module connectors, and common failure modes.

---

FPC (Flexible Printed Circuit) and FFC (Flat Flexible Cable) connectors are zero-height board-to-flex interconnects used in cameras, displays, hinges, and any assembly where a rigid PCB must connect to a flexible element. The distinction matters for sourcing: FPC connectors accept flexible printed circuits with etched copper conductors and a defined pad pitch; FFC connectors accept flat flexible cables with parallel conductors in a polymer carrier. The two are not always interchangeable even when pitch and pin count match — the insertion thickness and tab geometry differ between FPC and FFC variants within the same product family.

Both connector types use a surface-mount housing with an actuator (flip-lock, slide-lock, or back-flip) that clamps the flex or cable against the contacts once inserted. The fundamental design tradeoff is between contact force (which affects resistance stability) and actuation cycle life. ZIF (Zero Insertion Force) actuators require no force to insert the flex and then apply clamping force via the actuator — cycle life is typically 20–30 actuations, not hundreds. LIF (Low Insertion Force) connectors apply a small pre-load during insertion (0.2–0.5 N per contact) and do not require a separate actuator; they are simpler but less forgiving if the flex is inserted slightly misaligned.

The 20–30 cycle life of ZIF connectors is a design constraint, not a defect. It is adequate for manufacturing assembly and field replacement but rules out connectors in any mechanism that actuates repeatedly (daily hinge flex, rotating camera module). In those cases you need a different connector family (AFC-style with defined flex life) or a continuous flex design.
