EN 15194:2017 is the mandatory standard for electrically power-assisted cycles (EPAC) in the European Union. When sourcing from an electric bicycle manufacturer in China, verifying compliance is paramount. Key requirements: motor rated power must not exceed 250W, motor assistance must cut off when the rider stops pedaling or reaches 25 km/h, and the battery system must meet rigorous electrical safety requirements. A CE-marked e-bike without EN 15194 compliance documentation is not legally an EPAC — it falls under L-category motorcycle type approval instead, requiring registration, insurance, and a driving license, severely limiting your market for wholesale e-bikes.

EN 15194 compliance requires testing at an accredited notified body and costs approximately €1,500–3,000 per custom electric bike model. Leading Chinese factories supplying EU importers should already have this certification for their base frame and motor combinations. Our audit service verifies that the certification covers your specific battery-motor combination — changing from a 10Ah to 15Ah battery may void the existing type certificate and require re-testing, impacting your e-bike sourcing timeline.

The lithium-ion battery pack is the highest-risk component for e-mobility products, dictating the longevity and safety of your private label e-bikes. Samsung 21700 50E or LG M50T cells (3.6V, 5Ah) are the reference tier for branded wholesale buyers. In contrast, generic cells often lose 25–35% capacity within 200 cycles and generate inconsistent pedal-assist range claims. Always require your electric bike supplier to declare their cell provider and supply a cell spec sheet with cycle test data (capacity retention at 80% after 500 cycles minimum) to ensure high performance.
