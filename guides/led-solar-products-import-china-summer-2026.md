Summer drives a predictable spike in demand for outdoor LED and solar products: garden parties, camping trips, festivals, and home renovation projects that people defer all winter. That seasonal tailwind, combined with China's continued dominance in LED and solar manufacturing, makes this a practical category to source — if you pick the right sub-segments and run the numbers correctly.

This is not a list of things that merely exist in Chinese wholesale catalogs. Every category below is one where the unit economics work at realistic import volumes, the certification path is defined, and there is a clear reason why summer 2026 specifically matters. All seven sit inside our LED lighting sourcing practice, where the recurring failure points are battery cells, IP sealing, and photometric claims that don't survive third-party testing.

One context note before the products: US importers face stacked tariffs on most Chinese goods as of May 2026 — an MFN base rate of 0–3.9% plus a Section 301 surcharge of 25%, plus a Section 122 import surcharge of approximately 10% that is currently in effect until July 24, 2026 (at which point it may or may not be extended). That pushes the effective landed duty rate for most assembled LED fixtures to 35–39% of CIF value. Run your landed cost model before committing, not after.
