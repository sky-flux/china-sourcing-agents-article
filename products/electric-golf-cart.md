OEM electric golf carts from China are low-speed electric vehicles built on steel or aluminum chassis with AC or DC brushless motors, configurable as 2-, 4-, or 6-seat models for golf courses, resorts, industrial campuses, and last-mile logistics. Buyers typically work with a manufacturer that can customize body color, seating layout, battery chemistry, and motor power, then apply the certifications needed for the destination market. These carts sit at the intersection of automotive electronics and light industrial equipment, so the sourcing process overlaps with both vehicle compliance and fleet-management concerns. Lead times usually run 45–70 days for a 5-unit pilot and 60–90 days for a 50-unit fleet order after tooling and color confirmation.

When selecting an electric golf cart manufacturer in China, a 48V 100Ah LiFePO4 pack generally costs approximately $400–600 more upfront than an equivalent VRLA lead-acid pack. However, for wholesale electric golf carts, the economics quickly flip over a 3-year period: LiFePO4 delivers 2,000–3,000 charge cycles to 80% capacity vs just 400–600 for lead-acid. Furthermore, LiFePO4 maintains consistent performance across the entire discharge curve. Our sourcing service helps you evaluate total cost of ownership across battery chemistries and identify OEM golf cart suppliers with documented BMS testing procedures. For more on vehicle-grade electronics suitable for custom utility vehicles, see our automotive electronics industry page.

Lead-acid carts require monthly water top-up, terminal cleaning, and equalization charging — labor costs that add up in a large fleet. LiFePO4 is maintenance-free. For resort and hotel applications where maintenance staff availability is limited, LiFePO4 is the practical choice despite higher initial cost. Verify the BMS includes cell balancing: without balancing, LiFePO4 cells drift in capacity over time and one weak cell limits the entire pack.
