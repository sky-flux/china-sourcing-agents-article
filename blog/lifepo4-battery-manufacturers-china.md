---

Search "LiFePO4 battery manufacturers in China" and you will see two very different kinds of companies: the cell giants that make the actual lithium iron phosphate cells, and the thousands of pack assemblers that buy those cells, add a BMS, an enclosure, and terminals, then sell finished batteries. If you are sourcing 12 V RV batteries, solar storage racks, or industrial ESS cabinets, you usually need the second group — but the cells inside come from the first.

The cell-making tier is concentrated in Fujian (CATL in Ningde), Guangdong (BYD in Shenzhen, EVE in Huizhou), Anhui and Jiangsu (Gotion in Hefei, CALB in Changzhou, SVOLT in Changzhou), Zhejiang and Shanghai (REPT), and Fujian/Tianjin (Hithium in Xiamen, Lishen in Tianjin). Pack assembly is more dispersed: Dongguan, Shenzhen, and Zhongshan dominate the small-to-mid 12 V/24 V/48 V pack market, while larger energy storage integrators sit closer to the cell plants. Understanding this split saves you weeks of outreach to manufacturers who will never quote your order size.

The most expensive mistake in this category is not picking the wrong brand — it is paying A-grade prices for B-grade or refurbished cells. A pack factory can show you a CATL or EVE datasheet while using cells that failed the manufacturer's final sorting, then rewashed the QR codes and rewrote the cycle data. This article separates the battery cell manufacturers that matter from the pack assemblers that repackage them, and explains how to verify what you are actually buying.
