Agricultural drone sprayers, vital to modern precision agriculture and smart farming, occupy a distinct regulatory category from consumer and commercial UAVs. They are classified as RPAS (Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems) operating in agricultural airspace, and the approval framework differs significantly between China and export markets.

**CAAC RPAS Type Certificate (China).** The Civil Aviation Administration of China issues type certificates for agricultural drones under CCAR-92. All domestically sold crop protection drones must hold a CAAC type certificate for the specific airframe and payload configuration. This covers the aircraft structure's airworthiness, flight controller redundancy, and failsafe behavior (like return-to-home on link loss). **Note:** A CAAC type certificate is not a market access certification for EU or US markets—it strictly demonstrates Chinese regulatory compliance.

**EU Market: EASA Category A3 / Specific.** Heavy payload UAVs spraying chemicals fall under EASA category "specific" because they operate beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS). EASA's Specific category requires a SORA (Specific Operations Risk Assessment) completed by the operator. However, the drone must still hold CE marking under the EU Drone Regulation (EU 2019/945) for the UAS class category (C3 or C4). Ensure the Chinese manufacturer has the EU 2019/945 CE marking for the specific airframe and maximum takeoff mass (MTOM), not just generic electronics CE marks.
