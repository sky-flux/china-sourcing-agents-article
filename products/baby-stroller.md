An OEM baby stroller is a customizable wheeled child conveyance built by a Chinese contract manufacturer and sold under your brand. Most units use a 6061 aluminum alloy frame, EVA foam or air-filled PU wheels, a three-to-five-position recline, and a UPF 50+ canopy. You provide the logo, colorway, fabric selection, and feature changes; the factory handles tube cutting, TIG welding, fabric sewing, assembly, and export packaging. It is a low-voltage, non-electrical product, so compliance focus is entirely on mechanical safety and chemical safety rather than EMC or RF certifications.

Before you request quotes, lock the following in your specification sheet:

EN 1888-1:2018 replaced the previous EN 1888:2012 standard for wheeled child conveyances and baby carriages. It became mandatory for European Union market compliance in 2020. The key regulatory changes include much more stringent stability requirements (such as the topple test with a full newborn load including a loaded parent bag hook), updated restraint system strength tests for the 5-point safety harness, and extensively revised braking requirements. Our factory audit service strictly verifies that the Chinese stroller manufacturer's certification document references EN 1888-1:2018 explicitly — baby strollers certified only under the older EN 1888:2012 do not meet current EU market requirements and risk customs rejection.
