# Factory Audit Checklist: 47 Things to Verify Before Placing an Order

> **Read the full guide with updates**: [china-sourcing-agents.com/guides/factory-audit-checklist](https://china-sourcing-agents.com/guides/factory-audit-checklist)

Factory audits intimidate most buyers because they don't know what to look for. This checklist covers 47 specific things to verify — with explanations of why each matters.

**How to use this list**: Don't present it as a questionnaire — factories will give you the answers they think you want to hear. Ask open-ended questions and observe. "Show me your incoming inspection process" tells you more than "Do you do incoming inspection?"

---

## Section 1: Legal & Business (8 items)

1. **Business license validity** — Does the license cover manufacturing (制造业) or just trading?
2. **License registration scope** — Does the claimed product category match what's on the license?
3. **Company age** — How long have they been registered? Less than 2 years is a risk flag.
4. **Bank account name** — Does the bank account name match the company name on the license?
5. **Export license** — Do they have an export license, or do they use a freight forwarder's license?
6. **ISO 9001 certification** — Is it current? Which body issued it? Verify on the certification body's website.
7. **Product certifications** — FCC, CE, RoHS — are they actually held, or is the factory using a customer's certificate?
8. **Trade assurance or equivalent** — Do they participate in any third-party verification scheme?

---

## Section 2: Facility (10 items)

9. **Physical size vs. claimed capacity** — A 200m² factory claiming 50,000 units/month output is lying.
10. **Production line count** — How many SMT lines? Manual assembly lines? Wave solder?
11. **Worker count** — Ask for payroll records. Are the numbers physically plausible for claimed output?
12. **Material storage** — Is ESD-sensitive material stored properly? Is the storage area organized?
13. **Work-in-progress storage** — Is WIP labeled and tracked?
14. **Finished goods storage** — Is it separate from raw materials? Is it clean?
15. **Power supply** — Is there backup power? Electronics manufacturing needs stable power.
16. **Cleanliness** — Is the floor clean? Are tables organized? Dirty factories make dirty products.
17. **Lighting** — Is inspection lighting adequate? 1000+ lux for electronics assembly.
18. **Temperature/humidity control** — Required for component storage and SMT paste application.

---

## Section 3: Equipment (9 items)

19. **SMT line age** — Equipment older than 15 years is a risk; older than 20 years is a red flag.
20. **Solder paste printer** — Do they have an automatic printer, or are they hand-applying paste?
21. **Reflow oven profile capability** — Can they program and verify reflow profiles?
22. **AOI (Automated Optical Inspection)** — Do they have it? Is it in use or turned off?
23. **X-ray capability** — Required for BGA inspection. Do they have it in-house or outsource?
24. **Wave solder** — For through-hole components, is the wave solder properly maintained?
25. **Hand soldering stations** — Are they ESD-safe? Are iron tips replaced regularly?
26. **ICT/FCT test fixtures** — For your product specifically, do they have or will they build test fixtures?
27. **Calibration records** — Are test instruments calibrated? When was the last calibration?

---

## Section 4: Quality Systems (11 items)

28. **Incoming material inspection process** — Do they inspect components when they arrive?
29. **Incoming QC records** — Can they show you actual records from the past month?
30. **In-process QC** — Who checks quality during production? How often?
31. **Final inspection process** — What's their sampling plan? AQL 2.5 or 4.0?
32. **Defect rate records** — What's their reported defect rate? Is it credible?
33. **Customer complaint handling** — How do they track and respond to customer complaints?
34. **Non-conforming material process** — What happens to rejected components or products?
35. **Engineering change control** — How do they handle product changes? Do they notify customers?
36. **IPC-A-610 knowledge** — Do their QC inspectors know IPC-A-610? What class do they inspect to?
37. **Golden sample practice** — Do they use golden samples? Can they show you the current one?
38. **Traceability** — Can they trace a specific unit back to its production date and batch components?

---

## Section 5: Product-Specific for Electronics (9 items)

39. **ESD controls** — Are wristbands in use? Are mat ground straps connected?
40. **BT/WiFi module source** — Where do they source the radio module? Grey market is a risk.
41. **Battery source and documentation** — UN38.3 test reports for lithium batteries.
42. **Firmware flashing process** — How is firmware loaded? Is the version controlled?
43. **Product testing** — What do they test on 100% of units before packing?
44. **Waterproofing process** — For IPX-rated products, how is sealing verified? Pressure test?
45. **Cosmetic inspection criteria** — Do they have a written standard, or is it subjective?
46. **Packaging verification** — Is drop testing done? Carton marking compliance checked?
47. **Regulatory marking verification** — Is CE/FCC marking correct and complete on production units?

---

## Disqualifying vs. remediable findings

**Disqualifying** (walk away without remediation):
- Legal irregularities (items 1–4)
- ESD failures with no willingness to fix (item 39)
- Undocumented firmware process for connected devices (item 42)

**Conditionally remediable** (require written remediation plan before proceeding):
- Everything else — poor record-keeping, older equipment, weak quality processes can often be improved with explicit requirements in your purchase agreement.
