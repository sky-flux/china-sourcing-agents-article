Light quantity for plant growth is measured in photosynthetically active radiation (PAR) — photons in the 400–700nm wavelength range that drive photosynthesis. The three metrics that matter for specifying a grow light are PPFD, DLI, and photon efficacy.

**PPFD (Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density), μmol/m²/s.** The instantaneous photon flux density at a specific point, measured at a specific distance from the fixture. The relevant value for grow light specification is the average PPFD across the entire canopy footprint (not the peak center value, which some Chinese factories report). A 600W fixture at 600mm hanging height delivering 1,200 μmol/m²/s at center and 500 μmol/m²/s at corners has a very different agronomic performance than a fixture averaging 900 μmol/m²/s uniformly across the same footprint. Request a full PPFD map (grid measurement at 150mm spacing) from an independent photometric test lab, not the factory's own measurement.

**DLI (Daily Light Integral), mol/m²/day.** The total photon dose delivered per day = PPFD × photoperiod hours × 3,600 / 1,000,000. For indoor cultivation with 18h photoperiod at 600 μmol/m²/s: DLI = 600 × 18 × 3600 / 1,000,000 = 38.9 mol/m²/day. This is the metric that crop scientists use to correlate light to yield. Tomato require DLI 20–40 mol/m²/day for commercial yield; leafy greens 12–17 mol/m²/day. Specify the target DLI for your crop before calculating fixture power and spacing.
