Ningbo is one of China's most versatile sourcing cities, and it is consistently underestimated by buyers who only know it as a port. Yes, the Ningbo-Zhoushan Port — the world's busiest by cargo tonnage — sits adjacent to the factory clusters and provides the Yangtze Delta's most efficient container throughput. But the manufacturing base behind that port is broader than most buyers realize: Ningbo is simultaneously China's recognized Stationery Capital, one of the country's largest mold manufacturing bases, a significant hand tools and hardware cluster, and a growing electronics hub for consumer devices and PCB assembly.

For buyers attending the Canton Fair or sourcing across multiple product categories, Ningbo is worth treating as a primary destination rather than a logistics afterthought.

**Stationery and office products** are Ningbo's most distinctive category internationally. The city holds the formal designation as China's Stationery Capital and accounts for approximately 20% of China's total stationery output. Deli Group, headquartered in Ningbo, manufactures the full stationery range — ballpoint pens, mechanical pencils, staplers, hole punches, file folders, calculators, and desktop organizers — and exports to over 100 countries. Beifa Group, also Ningbo-based, is Asia's largest pen manufacturer by volume. For private-label buyers, this concentration means genuine OEM depth: most major stationery factories in Ningbo have dedicated export departments, CE and ASTM compliance documentation, and experience with retailer-specific packaging specifications. MOQs for stationery OEM programs typically start at 5,000 units per SKU.
