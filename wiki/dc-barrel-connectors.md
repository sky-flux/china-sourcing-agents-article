DC barrel power connectors (also known as DC jacks or DC plugs) are among the most commoditized electronic components in consumer electronics such as wireless chargers and portable power banks. Because they are so ubiquitous, Chinese electronics suppliers can deliver acceptable quality at a very low cost for most applications.

However, cheap DC power connectors come with well-understood failure modes: contact spring degradation, plastic housing deformation under thermal stress, and plating wear in high-cycle applications. The design decision that matters most is selecting the right size and variant for your power supply application upfront. For example, mixing 5.5/2.1mm and 5.5/2.5mm dimensions in a product line costs more to resolve in field returns than the entire connector bill of materials (BOM). This guide covers everything you need to know to source reliable DC barrel connectors from China.

Barrel connectors provide a separable DC power connection consisting of a cylindrical outer barrel (negative in center-positive convention) and a center pin (positive). The connector is specified by two dimensions: outer barrel diameter and center pin diameter, both in millimeters. The mating engagement is a friction-fit sleeve; locking requires an accessory threaded collar or spring-loaded retention mechanism. Barrel connectors are not a high-reliability interface — they are designed for consumer electronics with moderate insertion cycle requirements (typically 500–5000 cycles depending on variant). For industrial or high-cycle applications above 2000 insertions, specify a locking variant or switch to M12 industrial circular connectors.
