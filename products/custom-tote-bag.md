When sourcing from a **custom tote bag manufacturer in China**, OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certifies that every component of a textile product (fabric, thread, printing inks, zippers, buttons) has been tested for harmful substances and is harmless to human health. For reusable shopping bags, custom printed bags for baby items, or items given as gifts to families with children, skin contact is continuous — making OEKO-TEX a credible third-party safety claim. Major European retailers (Aldi, Lidl, Carrefour) actively include OEKO-TEX as a strict purchasing requirement for promotional textile items and corporate gifting. Our sourcing service identifies factories with valid OEKO-TEX certification that covers all components — not just the base fabric — and our audit service verifies the certification scope before you commit your budget.

The certification requires all components to be tested — not just the base fabric. Screen printing inks are a common failure point for many wholesale canvas bags: azo dyes that can release carcinogenic aromatic amines are banned under OEKO-TEX and EU REACH (EN ISO 14362-1). Always request OEKO-TEX certification documents that list the factory name, product description, and all components tested. A certificate covering only the fabric does not cover the inks, meaning your eco-friendly promotional products might still fail compliance checks.

RPET (recycled PET from plastic bottles) is increasingly demanded by ESG-conscious corporate buyers looking for a bulk tote bag supplier. GRS (Global Recycled Standard) certification provides necessary third-party verification of the recycled content percentage — without GRS, an "RPET" claim on promotional non-woven bags is unverifiable and potentially misleading. GRS certification is held by the fabric supplier, not the bag factory itself. Verify the factory sources fabric from a GRS-certified supplier and request the transaction certificates showing the supply chain traceability.
