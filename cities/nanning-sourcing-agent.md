---

Nanning is the capital of Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region and China's designated gateway to Southeast Asia. It sits 160 km from the Vietnam border and hosts the annual China-ASEAN Expo (CAEXPO) — the largest trade event between China and the ten ASEAN member states. Its manufacturing base is built around two industries that coexist nowhere else in China in the same concentration: a growing electronics assembly cluster and the country's largest sugar and agricultural processing industry. For buyers managing supply chains that connect China to Southeast Asia, or buyers sourcing both industrial equipment and agricultural commodities, Nanning's infrastructure serves functions that Pearl River Delta cities do not replicate.

Nanning's most distinctive economic function is as the administrative, logistics, and customs hub for China-ASEAN cross-border trade. The China-ASEAN Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA), which entered into force in 2010, provides zero or near-zero tariffs on most goods traded between China and ASEAN member states (Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Philippines, Singapore, Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos, Brunei). Nanning is where the customs infrastructure, bonded logistics zones, and freight forwarding expertise for CAFTA-eligible trade are concentrated.

The annual China-ASEAN Expo (CAEXPO), held in Nanning each September since 2004, draws over 2,000 exhibitors and 80,000+ buyers from across ASEAN and China. It is the primary trade matching event for China-ASEAN commercial relationships, covering consumer electronics, agricultural products, building materials, food processing equipment, and infrastructure goods. Buyers attending Canton Fair in Guangzhou in April and October should consider timing a Nanning factory visit around CAEXPO in September — the two events together cover the major trade channels between coastal Chinese manufacturing and ASEAN markets.
