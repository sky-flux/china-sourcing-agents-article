An agricultural drone sprayer is a remotely piloted aircraft system (RPAS) built to apply liquid crop-protection chemicals, fertilizers, or micronutrients over farmland. Unlike camera drones, these units carry 10–30L tanks, spray booms with centrifugal or hydraulic nozzles, and rely on RTK GPS to follow sub-meter flight paths without overlapping or skipping rows. For buyers, they are effectively flying field equipment: the airframe, flight controller, and spraying subsystem must all be validated as a single unit because a failure in any one part ruins an entire spray pass.

Agricultural drone sprayers occupy a distinct regulatory category from consumer and commercial UAVs. They are classified as RPAS operating in agricultural airspace, and the approval framework differs significantly between China and export markets.

**CAAC RPAS Type Certificate (China).** The Civil Aviation Administration of China issues type certificates for agricultural drones under CCAR-92. All domestically sold crop protection drones must hold a CAAC type certificate for the specific airframe and payload configuration. This covers the aircraft structure's airworthiness, flight controller redundancy, and failsafe behavior (like return-to-home on link loss). **Note:** A CAAC type certificate is not a market access certification for EU or US markets—it strictly demonstrates Chinese regulatory compliance.
