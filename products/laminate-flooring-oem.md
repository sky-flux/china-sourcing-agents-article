**EN 13329** is the rigorous European standard that strictly defines how commercial and residential laminate flooring panels are classified. The critical wear rating — ranging from AC1 through AC5 — is not determined by a single test result. Rather, it represents a composite pass/fail score across five distinct test methods: steel ball impact resistance, severe cigarette burn resistance, castor chair cycling durability, aggressive stain resistance to household chemicals, and most notably, the **abrasion class (Taber test, IP value)**. A wood-look laminate panel carrying an AC4 mark has successfully passed all five tests at the strict commercial light-use threshold. The vast majority of Chinese OEM factories comfortably manufacture to AC3 and AC4 specifications as their standard baseline. Reaching commercial-grade AC5 requires a much heavier aluminium oxide loading in the top surface overlay (typically 0.5–0.6mm thickness versus 0.2–0.3mm for standard AC3). This upgrade increases raw overlay material costs by roughly $0.30–0.50 per square meter but yields a far more durable floating floor.

**HDF core density** is widely considered the single most important structural variable for laminate planks, right after the top wear rating. While the EN 13329 test suite does not mandate a direct specific core density, this metric has a massive bearing on two critical performance properties that B2B buyers and end consumers care about: **acoustic impact sound deadening** and **point load dent resistance**. An HDF (High-Density Fibreboard) core weighing in at 880 kg/m³ will successfully absorb the sudden mechanical shock of a heavy chair leg or stiletto heel noticeably better than a lighter 820 kg/m³ board of the exact same thickness. When actively requesting bulk quotations from a laminate flooring China factory OEM supplier, explicitly demand the official density certification or the factory's internal press parameter records — do not rely solely on the nominal marketing specifications found on a standard product sheet. Minor density variations across a mass production batch of ±30 kg/m³ are perfectly normal; anything wider than that strongly indicates inconsistent pressing temperatures or heavily fluctuating moisture content within the raw HDF materials.

The **click joint system** profoundly matters for swift installation performance and for drastically reducing your legal liability exposure as a primary importer. Two dominant, heavily licensed systems rule the market: **Unilin click** (a massive Belgian patent portfolio now fully owned by Mohawk Industries) and **VALINGE 5G** (a renowned Swedish patent; the "5G" designation refers specifically to their ultra-fast fifth-generation fold-down angle-click mechanism). Both premium systems require Chinese flooring factories to hold a valid, active production licence and consistently pay per-square-metre manufacturing royalties. The crucial distinction that matters to wholesale buyers: **angle-click (fold-down)** is universally preferred for fast residential floating floor installations because it entirely eliminates the need for a traditional hammer and tapping block. The plank is smoothly inserted at an angle and pressed flat, a method that is remarkably forgiving on imperfect residential subfloors with minor height variations. Conversely, **drop-lock (vertical snap)** is significantly faster to install in highly controlled commercial settings but demands an exceptionally flat subfloor prep. If your primary target market is standard residential retail, strongly specify an angle-click mechanism directly in your purchase order.
